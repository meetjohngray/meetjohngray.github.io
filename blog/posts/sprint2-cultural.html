Sprint 2 Cultural

What is your long term goal and or career pathway?

I want to gain the skills needed for entry level employment in software development. Ideally this would be with a company or organisation that I feel aligns with my personal values and is making a positive contribution to the world. In the long term, I would love to be able to work remotely so that I could incorporate some long-term travel into my life. At this time I see myself more interested in front-end development, but I am also open to follow wherever my curiosity leads me. I also think I would enjoy and have some aptitude in project management.

What skills (non-technical - human skills) would you like to see developed in yourself while at EDA?

I would like more practice on how to work well with others, especially in the context of completing shared projects. I’d also like to work on being a good support/mentor for others in my cohort. As an older student, I feel I might have something to offer in this area. To do this well, I’ll have to manage my own feelings of overwhelm and identify in what areas I can be a valuable resource to my peers.

A description of your own strengths and limitations

  • I have succeeded at difficult tasks before: completing a semester long Outward Bound program, a 10-day silent meditation retreat, etc.
  • I pick up on the basics of things quickly
  • I generally get along with and work well with others
  • I am humble about what I don’t know
  • I have some good strategies for managing stress

Limitations

  • If things don’t come easily for me, I sometimes give up
  • I get bored with things quickly and want to move on to something else
  • I don’t think I’m very good at math
  • Despite my good strategies, stress can sometimes feel overwhelming to me

Based on the above description, a commitment to how you will manage your workload in this programme. This should include - an explanation of how you will manage yourself to work productively and safely with other learners, facilitators and industry/community representatives.

I am committed to putting in the time and effort required to complete this program. I am an early riser and will use those hours productively. I will manage my stress through running, yoga, and meditation. If I am feeling overwhelmed, I will step away to take a walk. I will use the experiences I’ve had in the past while working with others under stressful situations and use strategies the I have found effective such as listening, empathy, and humour. I will respect and value the opinions and backgrounds of those I come into contact with and try and always remember that everyone has something to teach me.

A commitment as to how and where you will seek help in a timely way.

I am committed to seeking help when needed from the teaching faculty, my fellow cohort members, and friends outside of the program. I will do this through one-on-one interactions, messaging software, and phone calls.

I expect teaching that will cater to the various different levels of knowledge and learning styles of my cohort. I expect timely feedback on my performance. I expect encouragement and honesty. I expect them to push me if they think I could be doing more.
